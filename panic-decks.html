The goal of this project was to create a set of 2 unique graphics for Panic Decks, a fingerboard company. I made each design by incorporating sharp, hand-drawn lines and transforming them into eye-catching visuals. Using my own original illustrations as a foundation, I enhanced them with digital graphic effects in Photoshop, experimenting with high-contrast textures and blending techniques. The result is a powerful visual identity for the brand.

process.

I began by sketching several rough concepts for the Panic Decks graphics, focusing on bold, abstract designs that would capture the brand’s identity. I used sharp, hand-drawn lines as the core of each design, creating a grainy look and feel. These sketches were then digitized using Procreate and further enhanced with Photoshop and Illustrator, where I applied high-contrast effects and intricate textures. The final graphics blend my hand-drawn elements with digital techniques, resulting in a unique aesthetic for the brand.

The final set of designs was refined through experimentation with different colorways, textures, and background techniques, creating a versatile range of graphics. After completing the designs, I shared them with the client, who provided positive feedback, confirming that they aligned perfectly with the brand’s visual identity.
